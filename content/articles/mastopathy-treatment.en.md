Dear patients,

mastopathy — benign changes in breast tissue — is a very common condition that worries many women. In most cases it is not life-threatening, but it does require proper diagnostics and supportive treatment.

I am pleased to offer you sensitive, professional counseling and treatment. Together, we will clarify your symptoms, perform the necessary examinations, and find the optimal approach for you.

## What to expect

### Diagnosis of mastopathy

- **Detailed medical history** (symptoms, duration, relationship to the menstrual cycle)
- **Clinical breast examination** (palpation to detect lumps and discharge)
- **Breast ultrasound** (assessment of tissue structure)
- **Mammography** (for women over 40)
- **Biopsy** (if necessary, to exclude cancer)

### Symptoms of mastopathy

- Breast pain (mastalgia), especially before menstruation
- Lumps in the breast
- Nipple discharge (clear, bloody, or milky)
- Breast swelling and edema
- Discomfort when wearing a bra

### Treatment methods

- **Conservative therapy** (vitamins, anti-inflammatory medications)
- **Hormone therapy** (if needed, to regulate the cycle)
- **Local treatment** (creams and gels for pain relief)
- **Lifestyle changes** (reducing caffeine, proper nutrition, stress reduction)
- **Supporting bra** (proper breast support)

### Breast self-examination

I will teach you the correct technique:

- How to palpate the breast systematically
- When to perform self-examination (usually one week after menstruation ends)
- What to look for and when to see a doctor
